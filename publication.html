The Effect of Realistic Appearance of Virtual Characters in Immersive Environments - Does the Character’s Personality Play a Role?


TVCG 2018

Katja Zibrek, Elena Kokkinara, Rachel McDonnell

Here, we explore if visual realism of virtual characters has an important effect on how their personality is perceived in virtual reality. We created a set of characters, ranging in render style (toon shaded to photo-realistic) and conceptual realism (zombie, creepy character) to explore the association between behavior and realism of virtual humans.

Does render style affect perception of personality in virtual humans?


SAP 2014

Katja Zibrek, Rachel McDonnell

People judge other people based on appearance all the time. Would we do the same with virtual characters? We tested how a character's personality is perceived when rendered in an appealing as oppose to a less appealing style. While mostly similar in other traits of the Big Five, an unappealing style could be perceived as less agreeable than an appealing one!

Exploring the effect of emotions on gender perception


SAP 2013

Katja Zibrek, Ludovic Hoyet, Kerstin Ruhland, Rachel McDonnell

People are usually very successful at recognizing gender from body motion alone. In our study, we argued that this ability varies strongly with the gender of the model that it is applied to, and interestingly enough - when characters express emotions, people start to make judgements about gender based on stereotypes that exist around emotions as well!
